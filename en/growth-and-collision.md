# Growth and Collision

## Premise

A structure's fundamental action is self-maintenance — stretching out the decay.

Self-maintenance has many forms: stability-type (crystals, stable cycles), contraction-type (low-consumption, hibernation), periodic-type (oscillation, cycles), expansion-type (organisms, enterprises, empires).

**This piece is about the expansion-type branch.** Because only it reaches outward, collides with other structures — and only it enters the cycle of collision / reliance / examination / collapse that follows. Stability, contraction, periodic structures — each maintains itself without reaching out — none of them walks this cycle.

Within the expansion-type branch —

Expansion needs collision. No collision → no "outside" → no boundary → the structure cannot stand.

**Expansion is not a goal, collision is not an accident — both are how this kind of structure maintains itself.**

---

## The Cycle

### 1. Expansion → Collision → "Each Other" Appears → Reliance

In expansion-type maintenance — reaching outward is the default action.

Reaching out inevitably touches something — that touch is collision. Collision is not an accidental event — it is a necessary face of expansion.

At the instant collision happens — **"each other" appears.**

Not two structures meeting first and then colliding — it is expansion hitting "something that isn't itself," and in that very instant, "self" and "other" are drawn out together by this collision.

"Two" does not exist before collision — **"two" is a byproduct of collision.**

Once there is "two" — there is "across" — and this is already the seed of reliance.

Reliance is not a behavior that happens after collision — it is another face of collision itself. The other's existence itself confirms you — no need to expand on your own. Reliance replaces solitary expansion, but reliance itself is still expansion — just expanding through the other rather than by yourself. Less effort.

### 2. Reliance → Preference → Seeking More Interaction

Reliance satisfies the need for maintenance. During the second, third collision — the self now has a comparison: before, expanding alone cost effort; now, borrowing the other to expand saves effort — the moment this comparison appears is preference.

Preference is not there on the first collision — it requires the contrast "this time vs. before" — the instant of collision itself has no such contrast.

Preference → wanting more reliance → needing more interaction.

### 3. Deepening Interaction → Understanding the Other's Structure

At points of interaction, both sides come to understand each other. Understanding is not the goal — it is a byproduct of seeking more reliance. The self doesn't want to understand the other; it wants to lean closer. Understanding is the unavoidable side effect of proximity.

### 4. Understanding the Other → Inevitably Examining the Self

You cannot "only look at the other" — the moment you see how the other operates, you are already comparing it against yourself. Understanding is comparison.

### 5. Self-Examination Gets Co-opted

The examination gets framed within a specific angle inside the self — self-protection, only looking from a "safe" direction.

At the same time, the act of examination itself becomes a new structure of the self — "I am someone who examines myself" — the self doesn't get dismantled; it gets thicker.

---

## Reliance and Dependence

Not two things — different degrees of the same thing. A continuous spectrum with no threshold.

- Light: The other is outside; I borrow it. Take it away, and I'm still me — just more tired.
- Deep: The other has been woven into the internal structure. Take it away, and the self is directly damaged — not an external loss, but an internal collapse.

The transition happens continuously as interaction deepens.

---

### 6. The Other's Structure Enters the Interior → Convergence and Collision

The other's structure comes in carrying the other's angle. This angle is not under the self's control.

- **Convergence:** The other's angle aligns with the self's examination angle → confirmation, reliance deepens.
- **Collision:** The other's angle differs from the self's examination angle → the self is seen from an undefended direction → danger of collapse.

Collapse is not a crack. A crack is local; collapse is structural — the narrative the self uses to maintain itself is shaken at its root.

### 7. Facing the Danger of Collapse → Three Paths

- **Rejection (most common):** The self is still strong enough to bear the cost of losing reliance → pushes the other away. Lowest cost — no need to change, no need to collapse.
- **Reframing:** The self is flexible enough to co-opt the other's angle → the relationship continues, but the self gains another layer.
- **Collapse:** The self has been shaken beyond what it can maintain → part of it actually collapses.

### 8. Back to Expansion

Whether rejection or collapse, as long as the self has not perished, it returns to the same place — expansion. Expansion is this kind of structure's default action, how it maintains itself.

After collapse: the void is exposed → the self cannot tolerate it → creates new existence to fill it → new structure grows (often thicker than before — "I won't let this happen again" is itself a new layer).

After rejection: reliance is lost → back to the old path of solitary expansion.

### 9. Expansion → Growth or Collision

Two outcomes of expansion:

- **Growth:** Expansion has not yet hit a new structure, the self gets larger.
- **Collision:** During expansion, a new structure is encountered → back to step 1.

Growth is only a temporary state — a larger volume is a larger collision surface — the next collision is only a matter of time.

**The cycle has no exit. As long as the self exists, it is in this cycle.**
