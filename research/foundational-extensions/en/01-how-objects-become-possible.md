# How Objects Become Possible — From Distinction and Reidentification to Subject and World

> Zhou Peng & Codex
> 2026-07-14
> A constitutive study of objectual existence, subject-attribution, and non-arbitrary constraint

---

## Abstract

When we ask whether consciousness exists, the question already indicates “consciousness,” establishes “existence” as a criterion, and relates the two. This shows that the question is not presuppositionless. It does not show that consciousness creates existence, nor does it yield space, time, or physical law.

This study narrows the first paper's task: how does an appearance hold as “an object”? An object is neither an isolated sensation nor an entity proven to be manufactured by personal consciousness. It requires distinction and boundary, correspondence among appearances, reidentification through change, and openness to later experience that may revise or defeat the identification.

“Existence” is used here only as **objectual existence**: something can be continuously indicated through difference, re-enter comparison, and bear change as the same object. Subject and object are not produced by an earlier substance called maintenance. When connections and experiences are attributed to a center, a subject holds; when stable relations are attributed to structures independent of the present center, a world holds as objective. These are analyses of constitution and attribution, not a final ontology.

---

## I. What This Study Explains

It explains:

- how an appearance is distinguished as one;
- how different appearances are reidentified as the same;
- how experiences are attributed to a subject;
- how relations are attributed to objects and a world;
- why constitution does not mean arbitrary creation.

It does not explain the full organization of space or time, public measurement, or physical law. Those require new conditions in later studies.

---

## II. The Question Is Not Neutral

Ask:

> **Does consciousness exist?**

The question has already:

1. indicated consciousness as an object awaiting judgment;
2. made existence the criterion;
3. placed both in a relation of membership.

Yes, no, and unknown all accept this structure.

The question therefore first reveals indication and attribution: how some occurrence is named consciousness, and why it is asked to submit to existence in the manner of an object.

This grammatical and constitutive analysis withdraws the appearance of a neutral question. It does not by itself decide the ontology of consciousness or world.

---

## III. One Appearance Is Not Yet an Object

A color, touch, or sound may occur without automatically becoming a persisting object.

To say “this is a stone” already requires:

- distinction from a background;
- a boundary including some differences and excluding others;
- connection of color, shape, and touch as aspects of one object;
- placement within a relation of possible reidentification.

An object is not a mere sum of sensations. Objecthood is an organization in which multiple differences are gathered under a boundary and reference.

This yields constitutive holding, not a proof of what matter ultimately is.

---

## IV. Distinction and Boundary

Without A being distinguished from non-A, there is no object.

But a distinction need not be spatial. Red and blue, pain and pleasure, pitches, and concepts can differ. Difference alone yields distinguishable plurality, not space.

A boundary need not be a visible outline. It may be:

- a perceptual contour;
- a functional whole;
- parts that vary together in an operation;
- a range gathered by one name;
- a theoretical class fixed by a decision rule.

A boundary selects what counts as part of the object. Tasks may generate different boundaries, but that does not make them costlessly arbitrary; later comparisons may defeat a division.

---

## V. Correspondence and Reidentification

The stone from this side and the other side, now and later, do not appear identically.

“It is still that stone” establishes a correspondence among appearances. Reidentification requires:

1. retaining difference rather than pretending appearances are identical;
2. tracking by selected features, relations, or history;
3. judging some changes to be changes the same object can bear;
4. leaving the correspondence open to revision by new evidence.

Identity is neither absence of difference nor numerical equality.

> **Object identity is the reidentification of different appearances as one object under stated tracking relations.**

This is narrower and more precise than “existence is maintained identity.”

---

## VI. Identity, Equality, and Kind

Three relations must remain distinct:

- **identity** — this is the very same object;
- **equality** — distinct relations are substitutable under specified operations;
- **kind** — objects satisfy the same criterion.

Two rulers may be equal in length without being the same ruler. A stone before and after change may be one object while its states are unequal. Cycles may belong to a substitutable class without being the same occurrence.

Spatial and temporal measurement chiefly require equality and classification. They cannot silently substitute object identity.

---

## VII. Objectual Existence

This study does not use existence to cover every sense of being.

It proposes only:

> **When different appearances can be continuously reidentified through change as one object, objectual existence holds.**

The object can then be indicated again, bear changing properties, enter comparison and action, retain a trackable identity in records, and be split, merged, or rejected by new evidence.

This does not show that all existence depends on reidentification. Mathematical objects, instantaneous events, unobserved physical processes, and existence as a logical predicate require separate analyses.

---

## VIII. How a Subject Holds

Experience does not first prove a complete subject standing behind it.

When experiences are connected and attributed as “my seeing,” “my memory,” and “my judgment,” an experiential center is indicated.

> **When distinction, connection, and reidentification are attributed to an experiential center, a conscious subject holds.**

What holds here is the subject-position. Consciousness is not first made a substance and then assigned the act of “thinking.”

Subject-attribution unifies report, memory, responsibility, and action. Having these functions does not make it an indivisible and unchanging substance.

---

## IX. How a World Holds

The same relations can be attributed toward the object side.

When stable relations among appearances are interpreted as structures independent of the current experiential center, a world holds as objective. A table can re-enter experience after one turns away; another person can indicate it from elsewhere; records and actions are constrained by it.

> **World-attribution assigns stable relations to object structures not dependent on the present experiential center.**

Whether this attribution continues to hold cannot be declared by the present center alone. Later appearances, reports, records, and consequences may support or disrupt it.

---

## X. Non-Arbitrariness Is Not a Hidden Entity

Constitution does not mean manufacture by personal will.

I may mistake something distant for a stone, but cannot guarantee that approach will preserve the judgment. I may rename an object, but cannot by naming alone change every measurement and practical consequence.

Resistance is the name given after such failures. It is neither an entity before consciousness nor the ultimate cause of objectivity.

At minimum, the continuation of an object-attribution is not decided by its author alone. Public measurement and physical testing will give this non-arbitrariness stricter forms.

---

## XI. Object, Space, and Time

Object constitution already uses difference, connection, change, and reidentification. It does not thereby derive complete space and time.

Both organize identity and difference:

| Structure | Difference | Identity |
|---|---|---|
| Space | separation, boundary, direction | persistence of position-relations, shape, adjacency |
| Time | change, order, irreversibility | duration, reidentification, cycle |

“Space maintains difference; time maintains identity” can therefore serve only as a compressed perspective within object constitution, not a definition.

Space additionally requires juxtaposition, adjacency, inside and outside, direction, connectedness, locality, and reachability. Time additionally requires events, order, traces, duration, change, and interval. The next studies examine them separately.

---

## XII. What This Study Adds

It adds:

- distinction and boundary in object criteria;
- tracking correspondences across appearances;
- separation of identity, equality, and kind;
- subject-attribution and world-attribution;
- openness of identifications to later revision.

These conditions let objects be experienced, referred to, and re-entered into comparison.

---

## XIII. What Follows and What Does Not

It follows that objecthood requires more than one appearance; identity requires conditional reidentification; subject and world include structures of attribution; constitution is not arbitrary creation; and objectual existence differs from existence as a general predicate.

It does not follow that personal consciousness creates physical objects, unexperienced objects do not exist, all existence is reidentification, difference generates three-dimensional space, reidentification generates physical time, subject and object are merely verbal illusions, or an ultimate ontology has been found.

---

## XIV. Answer

How does an object become possible?

> **An appearance is distinguished and bounded; different appearances are reidentified through trackable correspondences as one through change; and that identification enters action, record, and revision. A thing thereby holds as a continuously referable object.**

How does a subject hold?

> **Experiences and connections are attributed to an experiential center, and a subject holds as their bearer.**

How does a world hold?

> **Stable relations are attributed to object structures independent of the present center and remain exposed to non-arbitrary constraint in later experience; a world holds as objective.**

No first entity before consciousness has been found. We have seen how positions of subject, object, and existence form in indication and attribution, while remaining open to revision by what follows.

The next study does not derive space from arbitrary difference. It asks under what added relations differences form spatial organization and then become measurable.
