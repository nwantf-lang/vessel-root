# What It Means to Hold — Method and Boundaries of the Extension Studies

> Zhou Peng & Codex
> 2026-07-14
> A methodological note distinguishing constitution, expression, physical constraint, and ontology

---

## Abstract

The root texts of `vessel-root` and these extension studies perform different kinds of work. The root texts withdraw the experiencer, object, and existence when they are treated as absolute starting points. The extension studies ask how objects, space, time, public measurement, and physical laws can still work locally without restoring those absolute positions.

The series therefore does not deduce spacetime or physics from “consciousness thinks there is a conscious subject.” That sentence supplies no spatial axioms, temporal scale, rigid ruler, stable period, synchronization protocol, or law of nature. It prevents inquiry from quietly turning a subject, object, or theory into an unexplained final ground.

This study establishes a language discipline for what follows. It distinguishes four senses of “holding,” separates constitutive conditions from physical causes, requires every added condition to be explicit, and asks each construction to state its operations, constraints, modes of failure, and limits.

---

## I. Two Kinds of Text

### 1. Root texts that withdraw

A root text:

1. locates a position treated as an absolute beginning;
2. shows how that position is constituted in observation, naming, or attribution;
3. withdraws its absoluteness;
4. does not replace it with another entity.

“Consciousness thinks there is a conscious subject” is not a cosmogenesis. It does not narrate a pre-existing substance called consciousness producing a subject and then the world. It shows that when experience is attributed to a center, a subject is constituted as its bearer; when manifestation is indicated as “a thing,” an object-position is constituted as well.

“This is how all things come to be” concerns thinghood and attribution. It does not mean that personal consciousness manufactures the physical world.

### 2. Extension studies that construct

The extension studies examine local structures:

- how an object is distinguished and reidentified;
- how spatial relations are organized and measured;
- how temporal relations are retained and counted;
- how people, instruments, and records form public relations;
- how models become physical laws through prediction and failure.

Constructive studies may add conditions, but they must say so. They may not present those additions as logical consequences of the root sentence, nor may a final withdrawal excuse weak intermediate reasoning.

The relation between the two bodies of work is therefore:

> **The core documents withdraw absolute subjects and objects; the extension studies examine how local objects, measurement, and physical knowledge can still hold without restoring those absolutes.**

This is a study of compatibility, not a generative deduction.

## II. Four Senses of “Holding”

Without distinctions, one word allows an epistemic conclusion to slide into an ontological one.

### A. Constitutive holding

Something can be distinguished, connected, reidentified, or classified in experience.

Example: different appearances are reidentified as one stone, so object identity holds within experience.

### B. Representational holding

A relation can enter a concept, operation, number, coordinate, or equation.

Example: the relation between two boundaries is expressed as a length by a unit and a count.

### C. Physical holding

An expression places non-arbitrary constraints on later results across subjects, instruments, and conditions. It can succeed or fail.

Example: a clock model is not merely coherent; it must converge, within stated uncertainty, with independent clocks, signal paths, and phenomena not used in its calibration.

### D. Ontological holding

A claim says what something actually is, whether it exists independently of observation, or whether it is an ultimate structure of the world.

The first three do not automatically yield the fourth. Reidentification is needed to experience a stone as the same object; it does not follow that the stone's physical existence is personal reidentification. Measurement is needed for a numerical distance; it does not follow that no physical spatial relation existed before measurement.

---

## III. A Constitutive Condition Is Not a Physical Cause

“Without X, Y cannot be experienced or expressed as Y” is a constitutive analysis.

“X physically produces Y” is a causal claim.

They are not interchangeable. A trace lets an order be retained, recognized, and counted in a present structure; that alone does not show that traces create physical precedence. Units and comparisons constitute numerical length; that does not show that measuring creates every physical relation between objects.

This series studies how relations can be organized, expressed, and publicly tested. Unless a further argument is supplied, it does not answer what ultimately causes the world.

## IV. A Working Definition of Maintenance

“Maintenance” is no longer a hidden mechanism operating before consciousness, space, time, and existence. It is a working umbrella for five moments within already differentiated relations:

1. **distinction** — A is separated from non-A;
2. **correspondence** — appearances or positions are connected;
3. **equivalence** — different items become substitutable under stated conditions;
4. **trace** — a difference from one occurrence remains available to comparison;
5. **constraint** — an established correspondence is not guaranteed to continue; a new result may defeat it.

Its minimal definition is:

> **Maintenance is the process-structure in which correspondences among differences are selectively retained, re-entered, and kept open to failure.**

This is not a description of something “before consciousness.” Distinction, re-entry, process, and failure already belong to a differentiated structure. The definition analyzes unfolded experience and inquiry.

## V. Non-Arbitrariness and Failure

Objectivity cannot mean internal coherence alone. A dream, a formal system, or a game world may be stable, coherent, and coordinate-transformable.

Physical inquiry additionally requires:

- convergence of independent paths within stated uncertainty;
- transformation loops that close without arbitrary residue;
- extrapolations that risk failure under new conditions;
- discrepancies that cannot be absorbed costlessly by changing every rule;
- data and interventions capable of narrowing, revising, or defeating a model.

Constraint or resistance is not an earlier substance. Only after descriptions, results, and comparisons exist can a mismatch be named failure and what is not decided by the present description be named non-arbitrary constraint.

At minimum:

> **Subjectivity appears where a relation is established from a local position; objectivity appears where the public continuation of that relation is not decided by any one position alone.**

This characterizes an operating structure. It is not a first cause.

## VI. Six Questions for Every Study

Each study must answer:

1. What does it explain?
2. What does the preceding work already provide?
3. What new conditions does it add?
4. How can those conditions be operated, reproduced, compared, and challenged?
5. What follows from them?
6. What does not follow from them?

The additions matter. Spatial measurement adds allowed transformations, comparison procedures, stable carriers, and calibration. Temporal measurement adds event criteria, equivalent cycles, counting, signals, and synchronization. Physical law adds variable selection, models, mathematics, prediction, and testing. None follows by pure logic from the root sentence.

---

## VII. The Revised Research Path

The series contains one methodological study and five substantive studies:

> **00 What It Means to Hold** — method, language, and boundaries.
> **01 How Objects Become Possible** — distinction, boundary, reidentification, subject attribution, and objectual existence.
> **02 How Space Is Organized and Measured** — from juxtaposition, adjacency, direction, and reachability to equal length, units, and coordinates.
> **03 How Time Is Organized and Measured** — from events, order, traces, and change to cycles, counting, and synchronization.
> **04 How Public Objectivity Becomes Possible** — from local experience to instruments, calibration, transformation closure, and independent convergence.
> **05 How Laws Become Possible** — from variables and models to invariants, prediction, counterfactuals, and failure.

This is not a single deduction from consciousness to physics. Each step inherits earlier structures and openly adds local conditions.

---

## VIII. General Boundary

The series does not claim that:

- personal consciousness creates the physical universe;
- all existence is object reidentification;
- arbitrary difference automatically produces space;
- pure identity automatically produces time;
- coherence or transformability by itself proves physical reality;
- metrological conventions uniquely determine world structure;
- finite measurement establishes an ultimate ontology.

It asks how observation can retain discipline after an absolute observer is withdrawn, and how object relations can remain non-arbitrary after an absolute object is withdrawn. Through public conditions, transformable expressions, independent tests, and risks of failure, local constructions can become knowledge that no single perspective owns.
