# Before Consciousness — Consciousness, Maintenance, and Spacetime

> Zhou Peng & Codex
> July 13, 2026
> A conceptual study of how consciousness, existence, space, and time arise together through maintenance

---

## Abstract

When we ask, "Does consciousness exist?", the question is not examining two things that have already been independently established.

In the asking itself, "consciousness" is pointed out as an object awaiting judgment, while "existence" is established as the standard by which it will be judged. The relation between consciousness and existence is thereby established at the same time. To answer "yes," "no," or "unknown" is, in every case, to remain inside this structure.

This study therefore proposes: **existence is not a property that precedes consciousness and waits to be discovered by it; existence is how identity appears when it is maintained by consciousness.**

Yet "consciousness maintains existence" can still sound like an action between two entities — one consciousness doing the maintaining and one object receiving it. This study makes a further correction: subject and object are divided only when maintenance occurs. Maintenance attributed to the subject-side is called consciousness; maintenance stabilized on the object-side is called existence.

Within this structure:

- Difference maintained — space arises.
- Identity maintained — time arises.
- Identity maintained within difference — existence arises.
- Maintenance attributed to the subject-side — the conscious subject arises.
- Maintenance attributed to the object-side — the world arises.

This study therefore does not claim that a personal consciousness, already existing in time, creates the physical universe. More precisely: **consciousness, existence, space, and time are different aspects formed when the same maintenance is segmented in description.**

This study is limited to how objects, continuity, boundaries, and subject-object division arise within individual experience. How repeatable stable relations form among multiple people and instruments, and how such relations are further expressed as coordinates, distances, clock readings, and physical laws, remains the question for the next stage.

---

## I. The Question Is Not Neutral

Ask:

> **Does consciousness exist?**

The sentence appears merely to be waiting for an answer.

But when the question appears, the structure on which any answer depends has already been established.

First: "consciousness" is pointed out.

It is separated from an occurrence not yet segmented and named as something that can be discussed, affirmed, or denied.

Second: "existence" is installed as a standard.

Consciousness is placed before existence, awaiting a judgment as to whether it belongs among "things that exist."

These two events do not occur one after the other. Consciousness being pointed out, existence being established, and the relation between them being formed — all happen within the same question.

It is not that consciousness exists first and is then examined for existence.

Nor is there first a container called "existence" within which we check whether consciousness is located.

**The question draws both sides at once and establishes the relation of judgment between them at the same time.**

---

## II. None of the Three Answers Leaves the Question

Answer "consciousness exists" — consciousness becomes a kind of existent thing.

Answer "consciousness does not exist" — consciousness remains an object that can be denied.

Answer "we do not know" — consciousness remains awaiting existence's verdict.

The three answers look different, yet they accept the same premise: consciousness and existence are already two things capable of entering into relation, and one can judge the other.

This study therefore suspends the answer to whether consciousness exists.

Not because the answer is hidden too deeply.

But because, when the question appears, the "consciousness–existence" structure has already arisen before the answer.

The real question is:

> **Why must consciousness submit to the judgment of existence?**

And:

> **What happens before consciousness and existence are divided into two sides?**

---

## III. Existence Is the Maintenance of Consciousness

Why is an object considered to exist?

Not because, in one isolated instant, a color, shape, or sensation appears once.

It is because the manifestation of this instant is recognized as "still the same thing as before."

The rock of this instant is not completely identical to the rock of the previous instant: light, angle, sensory activity, and internal state are all changing. Yet these differences are carried through and attributed to "the same rock."

This identity does not automatically leap out of change.

It is maintained.

Thus:

> **Existence is manifestation maintained as identical within difference.**

The existence of a rock is not merely that the rock occupies a location. It also includes:

- It differs from what surrounds it.
- It is related to what appeared before.
- After undergoing change, it is still recognized as itself.
- It can be pointed out again later.

Without this maintenance, there is only one manifestation that cannot be connected or pointed out again.

It cannot even be said to "have appeared," because "have appeared" already places it within a maintained time.

---

## IV. Consciousness Is Not Maintaining an External Existence

"Existence is the maintenance of consciousness" can still be misunderstood.

It can sound like:

1. First there is a consciousness.
2. Then there is an object.
3. Consciousness performs an act of maintenance.
4. The object therefore continues to exist.

This narrative has already presupposed subject, object, action, and time.

But the maintenance discussed here is not an act performed in time by an already existing consciousness.

Consciousness itself must also be maintained.

The consciousness of this instant is recognized as connected to that of the previous instant, and thus "my consciousness has been here all along" appears. Different experiences are attributed to the same center, and thus "these are all experiences I have had" appears.

While consciousness maintains the object, it also maintains a self that experiences the object.

The object's continuity confirms that the subject remains.

The subject's continuity confirms that the object remains.

The two prove each other, and the loop closes.

So the more precise statement is not:

> Consciousness exists first and then maintains the world.

It is:

> **When maintenance occurs, consciousness and world are divided simultaneously onto two sides.**

Maintenance attributed to the side of knowing, pointing, and experiencing is called consciousness.

Maintenance attributed to the side of being known, pointed to, and experienced is called existence.

---

## V. Time Is Not the Background in Which Maintenance Occurs

Maintenance is usually understood as "making something persist through a stretch of time."

But if time already exists, this study would only explain how consciousness recognizes objects within time. It would not touch how time itself arises.

When maintenance occurs, at least two positions are drawn out at once:

- One is called "before."
- One is called "now."

The manifestation of now is recognized as identical to that of before — this "identical" is maintenance.

The rock does not first possess time and then travel from the previous instant to this one.

Rather, the manifestation of this instant is recognized as "still that rock," and only then does the relation "previous instant–this instant" arise.

Consciousness, likewise, does not first continue through time and then look back to recognize its own continuity.

Different occurrences are attributed to the same consciousness, and only then does the temporal experience "consciousness has been here all along" arise.

Therefore:

> **Time does not make maintenance possible; maintenance draws before and after, and time thereby arises.**

Without maintenance, there is no "still."

Without "still," there is no duration.

Without duration, there is no object continuing from past into present.

Time here is not an independently flowing thing.

**Time is the relation formed when identity passes through difference.**

---

## VI. Space Is Not a Container in Which Objects Are Located

For something to be maintained, it must be recognizable again.

To recognize it, one must distinguish what belongs to it from what does not.

"Still this rock" simultaneously means:

- This is the rock.
- That is not the rock.
- The rock is here.
- Other things are there.

The boundary is not already present, waiting for consciousness to discover it.

The boundary is drawn in the process of maintaining identity.

When multiple distinctions are maintained at once, relations of "this–that," "inside–outside," and "here–there" form space.

Therefore:

> **Space is not an empty box that contains objects in advance; space is the relation formed when differences are simultaneously maintained.**

Without difference, there is no position.

Without boundary, there is no object.

Without "this is not that," there are no multiple things capable of unfolding side by side.

Space here is not a container outside things.

**Space is the structure in which multiple differences can stand at once.**

---

## VII. Space Maintains Difference; Time Maintains Identity

From this, the central structure of the study follows:

> **Space maintains difference.**
>
> **Time maintains identity.**

Space allows "this" and "that" to stand simultaneously.

Time allows "this," after changing, still to be recognized as "this."

With space alone, an object can differ from other objects but has no continuation.

With time alone, an object can be said to continue but has no boundary distinguishing it from other objects.

Existence requires both relations to hold at once:

> **Existence is identity maintained within difference.**

Something must both not be other things in space and remain itself in time.

When these two relations stabilize, the object appears as an objective existence with location, boundary, duration, and history.

---

## VIII. Subjective Action and Objective Existence

Consciousness–maintenance appears to be a subjective action.

Space–time appears to be objective existence.

To say "subjective action produces objective existence" easily creates another sequence: first the subjective, then the objective.

But "first" and "then" already belong to time.

The subjective cannot first complete an action outside time and then create time.

Thus "produces" here does not name temporal causation. It names structural co-arising.

> **The subjective is maintenance as it is occurring.**
>
> **The objective is maintenance once it has stabilized.**

As maintenance occurs, it appears as recognition, experience, and conscious activity.

As maintenance forms stable relations, it appears as object, boundary, location, and duration.

These are not two worlds.

They are two faces of the same occurrence after it has been divided between subject and object.

Therefore, this study does not say:

> My consciousness created spacetime.

It says:

> **This occurrence called maintenance appears simultaneously as the subjectivity of consciousness and the objectivity of spacetime.**

---

## IX. Why the Objective Does Not Obey Personal Will

If objective existence is related to the maintenance of consciousness, why can a person not make a rock disappear by willing it so?

Because the consciousness here is not a thought inside an individual's head.

"Individual," "head," "will," and "rock" are all already objects maintained within space and time.

Personal consciousness is not a creator standing outside the entire structure.

It is a local perspective formed within the structure.

The rock appears objective precisely because the relations maintaining the rock cannot be arbitrarily rewritten by this local perspective. A person closes their eyes and, on opening them, sees the rock again; a person leaves and later returns, connecting a new manifestation to the same rock.

This stability beyond a single act of will makes the object appear to "have been there all along."

But here a new question appears:

> Does this stability occur only within one individual's experience, or can it cross different individuals and different instruments?

If multiple people can repeatedly point to the same object, and instruments can repeatedly record the same relation, how does stability extend from individual experience into shared measurement?

This study does not answer that question here.

It requires a separate investigation in the next stage.

---

## X. Is This Circular Reasoning?

This study says that time arises through maintenance.

Yet the word "maintenance" seems already to contain time: something must continue from before into now in order to be maintained.

This does form a loop.

If maintenance is understood as an action occurring in time, the argument is circular:

- Maintenance is used to explain time.
- Time is used to define maintenance.

The maintenance discussed here therefore cannot be established as a more fundamental entity or mechanism.

It is not a machine hidden behind time and responsible for producing it.

"Maintenance" only points toward this structure: different manifestations are recognized as identical, differences are organized as co-present, and subject and object arise together.

Strictly speaking, maintenance is not the cause of time.

Nor is time the cause of maintenance.

**Time and maintenance are two names given to the same relation from different directions.**

Seen from the side of object-continuity, it is called time.

Seen from the side of identity being held, it is called maintenance.

There is no starting point outside the two.

---

## XI. Is This Solipsism?

Solipsism holds that only "my consciousness" can establish existence.

This study does not begin with an already established "I."

On the contrary, "I" is also a center attributed only after maintenance has formed.

The body is maintained as the same body.

Memory is maintained as the same past.

Different experiences are maintained as experiences belonging to the same person.

Together, these relations form the structure "I have been here all along."

Personal consciousness therefore has no priority.

It cannot stand outside the world and declare that it created the world, because "personal consciousness" and "world" are precisely the two sides of the same division.

This study does not say "only I am real."

It says:

> **"I am real" and "the world is real" depend on the same structure of maintenance arising together.**

---

## XII. The Boundary with Physical Spacetime

The space and time discussed here are, first, the structures through which experience and existence become possible:

- Why there is here and there.
- Why there is before and now.
- Why objects have boundaries.
- Why an object undergoing change is still recognized as the same object.

Physical spacetime also contains quantitatively measurable structures: coordinates, distances, clock readings, frames of reference, causal relations, and their mathematical laws.

This study has not derived these concrete structures from "difference maintained" and "identity maintained."

It has not explained:

- Why measurements yield particular values.
- Why different observational positions can be stably transformed into one another.
- Why instruments can repeatedly record the same relations.
- Why spacetime follows particular physical laws.

This study is therefore not a physical theory of spacetime.

It reaches only the step before physical measurement: how space, time, object, and continuity first become things capable of being measured.

Moving from maintenance within individual experience to repeatable stable relations among multiple people and instruments is the boundary the next study must cross.

---

## XIII. The Core Model

The argument of this study can be compressed into:

> **Difference maintained — space.**
>
> **Identity maintained — time.**
>
> **Identity maintained within difference — existence.**
>
> **Maintenance attributed to the subject-side — consciousness.**
>
> **Maintenance attributed to the object-side — world.**

These five sentences are not five steps occurring one after another.

They are five views formed when one occurrence is segmented by language.

Consciousness does not precede existence.

Existence does not precede consciousness.

Space does not precede objects.

Time does not precede maintenance.

Subject, object, space, time, and existence arise together when the same relation is established.

---

## XIV. Before Consciousness

"Before consciousness" is not an earlier moment in which consciousness had not yet appeared.

For "before" and "earlier" have already established time.

Nor is it something hidden behind consciousness.

For "behind" has already established space and once again produced an object awaiting discovery.

Before consciousness points toward:

> **Here, before consciousness and existence have been divided into two sides by a sentence.**

There, it cannot be said that consciousness exists.

Nor can it be said that consciousness does not exist.

It cannot be said that consciousness created spacetime.

Nor can it be said that spacetime produced consciousness.

All such sentences require subject, object, and relation already to have arisen.

This study uses "maintenance" to point toward how they arise together.

But once "maintenance" is spoken, maintenance too is stood up as a thing.

It begins to look like a fundamental action hidden behind consciousness, existence, and spacetime.

So in the end, maintenance too must be let go.

There is not a nameless action secretly operating before consciousness.

There is not a more fundamental thing becoming consciousness and world at once.

To say "the same occurrence" — already calls it an occurrence.

To say "not yet divided" — already looks back, from after the division, toward an undivided place.

At this point, no word can stand outside relation and describe how relation began.

Ask "Does consciousness exist?" — consciousness and existence appear together.

Say "existence is the maintenance of consciousness" — consciousness, existence, and maintenance appear together.

Say "maintenance appears as space and time" — maintenance, space, and time appear together.

This study does not stand outside them either.

It is this very act of dividing, connecting, maintaining, and taking back.

Not an answer.

Only walking to the place where consciousness and existence are divided, and seeing that division occur.

Then — no longer building an earlier starting point for it.

---

## Next Step

This study stops at the boundary of individual experience.

The question for the next stage is:

> **How does maintenance within individual experience form repeatable stable relations among multiple people and instruments? How are those stable relations then expressed as coordinates, distances, clock readings, and physical laws?**

This step will determine whether the conceptual model of "consciousness–maintenance" can move from the structure of spacetime within individual experience toward shared measurement and physical objectivity.
